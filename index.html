Abstract

Modal title

Abstact

Integrated learning is a project in which students are put into groups and assigned work that are collaborations between multiple subjects. This year's ninth-grade main IL project is running a booth for a day. Each group runs one booth, and each class holds a bazaar each day, depending on the schedule. Students are also required to open pre-orders, and some products can be sold after the bazaar if there are still some left. Students are actively being pushed out of their comfort zone. Students are assigned work that requires planning, promoting, selling, and making reports about the bazaar. Many studies have reported that most schools don't teach students about life skills for many reasons, including a rigid curriculum that all schools must follow. However, Saint Ursula Jakarta Junior High School has a solution for this. So, Integrated Learning (IL) was established to solve this problem.

We have made many preparations for the booth. We made the booth. “Monsters Inc." themed and called the booth “Monsters Mart.”. We made products such as our own designed t-shirts (two versions; black and white), our own designed tote bags, parfaits, cake pops, milo dino drinks, nachos, snack boxes, bracelets, and keychains. We also prepared a photo booth and charged customers to get Polaroid pictures. Apparently, there were a few products that were assigned to be selling foods that consist of biotechnology and another product of livestock or fishery products as its main ingredient. For biotechnology, we made parfaits made of fermented yogurt and cake pops made of homemade bread. For livestock or fishery products, snack boxes were made. A single snack box consists of chicken popcorn, french fries, and mozzarella sticks. These two products were graded differently based on the subject teachers’ wants. The snack box product will be made the morning before the bazaar, where the class gathers in the school kitchen.

We've encountered challenges throughout this project. One of them was having loads of pre-orders for the t-shirt product. We're so happy about it because our product was selling fast. However, we underestimated the number of pre-orders that we would get. Some t-shirt sizes and colors were not enough to fulfill all the pre-orders. Even so, we are still left with some t-shirts that no one bought.

As a solution, we made a public apology story post on Instagram, and thankfully no one complained. To order more of the lacking t-shirt products, the manufacturer stated that ordering 12 t-shirts at once was cheaper than buying the t-shirts one by one, and the number of t-shirts we have yet to deliver to buyers is less than 12. The cost of one t-shirt has a higher price than the price we sold these t-shirts for. So, we ordered 12 t-shirts at once. As for the remaining t-shirts, it was solved by selling these t-shirts to friends and family outside of school. After all that, we were ready to make our report.

Based on what had happened and what we've encountered, deep inside, there are values we could get. From the challenges we've encountered, we believe that teamwork is the most essential element of this project. Since the project starts with teamwork, it also needs to end with teamwork. By stating that every mistake and challenge we've encountered will all work out with the help of teamwork and communication, As stated above, skills such as entrepreneurship, teamwork, marketing, collaboration, problem-solving, money management, pricing, customer service, and so many more were improved while doing the IL project.
